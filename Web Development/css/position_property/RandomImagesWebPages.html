MOUNT HIMALAYAS.

The Himalayas are the world's tallest mountain range, formed by the collision of the Indian and Eurasian tectonic plates, and home to Mount Everest. Himalayas are located in Asia, that spans the border between India, Nepal, China, Bhutan, and Pakistan.

MOUNT FUJI.

Mount Fuji is famous for its iconic, near-perfect conical shape, its status as Japan's highest and most sacred mountain, and its deep cultural and spiritual significance, which has inspired centuries of art, literature, and pilgrimage.

OCEAN.

An ocean is a vast, interconnected body of salt water covering approximately 71% of the Earth's surface, divided into five major basins: the Pacific, Atlantic, Indian, Southern, and Arctic oceans.
